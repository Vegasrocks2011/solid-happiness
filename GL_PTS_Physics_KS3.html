Energy

Energy stores, transfers, calculations, domestic fuel use

Must know
Energy stores and transfers
Energy can be stored as: kinetic, gravitational potential, elastic potential, thermal, chemical, nuclear, electromagnetic. Energy is transferred by: heating, work done by forces, electrical work, radiation. Energy is always conserved — it cannot be created or destroyed.
Must know
Key energy equations
P = E ÷ t Power (W) = Energy (J) ÷ time (s)
E = P × t Energy = Power × time
1 kWh = 3,600,000 J
Cost = power (kW) × time (h) × price per kWh
Should know
Conduction, convection, radiation
Heat transfers from hot to cold. Conduction — through solids via particle vibration. Convection — through fluids via density differences (hot rises). Radiation — electromagnetic waves (infrared), no medium needed. Insulators reduce heat transfer.
Stretch
Simple machines
A machine can increase force but reduces the distance moved — the product (force × distance = work done) stays the same. A lever, pulley or ramp trades distance for force. Work done = force × distance.
🏃

Motion and Forces

Speed, distance-time graphs, forces, pressure, Hooke's Law

Must know
Speed equation
speed = distance ÷ time
Units: m/s or km/h. Average speed = total distance ÷ total time. On a distance-time graph: gradient = speed, horizontal line = stationary, steeper line = faster.
Must know
Forces — balanced and unbalanced
Forces are pushes or pulls, measured in newtons (N). Balanced forces → no change in motion. Unbalanced forces → acceleration or deceleration. Resultant force = sum of all forces in one direction (considering direction).
Must know
Hooke's Law
F = k × e Force (N) = spring constant × extension (m). Within the elastic limit, extension is directly proportional to force. Beyond the elastic limit, the spring is permanently deformed.
Must know
Pressure
P = F ÷ A Pressure (Pa) = Force (N) ÷ Area (m²). Pressure in liquids increases with depth. Atmospheric pressure decreases with altitude. Upthrust acts upward on objects in fluids.
Should know
Non-contact forces
Gravity, magnetism and static electricity all act at a distance without contact. Gravity always attracts. Magnets: like poles repel, unlike poles attract. Charged objects: like charges repel, unlike charges attract.
Stretch
Moments
Moment = Force × perpendicular distance (Nm). For a balanced lever: sum of clockwise moments = sum of anticlockwise moments. A longer lever arm gives a greater turning effect.
🌊

Waves

Sound waves, light waves, reflection, refraction, EM spectrum

Must know
Wave properties
All waves transfer energy without transferring matter. Transverse waves: vibration perpendicular to direction of travel (light, water). Longitudinal waves: vibration parallel to direction of travel (sound). Key terms: amplitude, wavelength, frequency, wave speed.
Must know
Sound waves
Sound requires a medium to travel — it cannot travel through a vacuum. Speed of sound in air ≈ 340 m/s (faster in solids and liquids). Frequency measured in Hz. Humans hear 20–20,000 Hz. Echoes are reflections of sound.
Must know
Light waves
Light travels as a transverse electromagnetic wave at 3×10⁸ m/s in a vacuum. Reflection: angle of incidence = angle of reflection. Refraction: light bends when it changes speed (e.g. glass). White light splits into a spectrum through a prism.
Should know
Ray model of light
Used to explain mirrors (plane and curved), the pinhole camera, refraction, and convex lenses. A convex lens converges (focuses) light. The retina detects light in the human eye. Cameras use a similar focusing mechanism.
🔌

Electricity and Electromagnetism

Circuits, current, resistance, static electricity, magnetism

Must know
Current, voltage and resistance
V = I × R Voltage (V) = Current (A) × Resistance (Ω). Current measured in amperes (A). Potential difference (voltage) measured in volts (V). Resistance measured in ohms (Ω).
Must know
Series and parallel circuits
Series: same current throughout, voltages add up, one break stops all. Parallel: current splits, same voltage across each branch, components work independently. Adding bulbs in parallel → each is brighter than in series.
Must know
Magnetism
Magnetic poles: N and S. Like poles repel, unlike poles attract. Magnetic field lines go from N to S outside the magnet. Earth has a magnetic field — compasses align with it. A current-carrying wire creates a magnetic field around it.
Should know
Static electricity
When objects are rubbed, electrons are transferred, leaving one positively charged and one negatively charged. Like charges repel; unlike charges attract. The electric field exerts force on charged objects without contact.
🧊

Matter

States of matter, particle model, density, Brownian motion, diffusion

Must know
Particle model of matter
Solid: particles in fixed positions, vibrate only. Liquid: particles close but can flow. Gas: particles far apart, move fast and randomly. Changes of state involve changes in particle arrangement and energy — no new substance formed.
Must know
Density
ρ = m ÷ V Density (kg/m³) = mass (kg) ÷ volume (m³). Solids are generally denser than liquids, which are denser than gases. Ice is less dense than water — an important anomaly. Objects float if less dense than the fluid.
Should know
Brownian motion and diffusion
Brownian motion: random movement of particles in a fluid due to collisions with surrounding molecules — evidence for the particle model. Diffusion: spreading of particles from high to low concentration. Faster at higher temperatures.
Stretch
Internal energy
Internal energy = kinetic energy + potential energy of all particles. Heating a substance increases particle kinetic energy (temperature rises) or does work to separate particles during a change of state (temperature constant).
🌌

Space Physics

Gravity, weight, orbits, the Solar System, stars, seasons

Must know
Weight and gravity
W = m × g Weight (N) = mass (kg) × gravitational field strength (N/kg). On Earth g = 10 N/kg. Mass is constant everywhere; weight changes with gravitational field strength. The Moon has a lower g than Earth.
Must know
The Solar System and beyond
Our Sun is a star. Planets orbit the Sun in ellipses, held by gravity. Our galaxy is the Milky Way — it contains billions of stars. There are billions of galaxies in the universe. Distance in space measured in light-years (distance light travels in one year).
Must know
Seasons and day length
Earth is tilted on its axis at ~23.5°. As Earth orbits the Sun, the tilt causes seasons — when the Northern Hemisphere tilts toward the Sun, it is summer there. Day length is longer in summer (more hours of daylight).
Should know
Gravity in space
Gravity acts between all objects with mass. The Moon orbits Earth due to Earth's gravity. Earth orbits the Sun due to the Sun's gravity. Gravity decreases with distance. Stars and planets form when gravity causes gas and dust to collapse inward.
